About Cloudberries

The cloudberry is a low growing, perennial herb in the raspberry family. It grows on moist soils and is known for it's fiery yellow fruit of the same name, each fruit is initially pale red, ripening into an amber color in early autumn. The fruit is rich in vitamin C. It grows between 10 and barely 30 centimeters high. Despite great demand as a delicacy (particularly in Sweden, Norway and Finland) the cloudberry is not widely cultivated and is primarily a wild plant. The cloudberries are mainly used to make jam, but also liquor and the leaves for tea.

Vanilla Ice cream with warm cloudberries

Vanilla ice cream

  1. 0.5l vanilla ice cream
  2. 1.5dl cream
  3. 1krm vanilla powder

Cloudberries

  1. 300g cloudberries
  2. 0.5dl granulated suger
  3. 2msk cloudberry liqueur

Do the following:

  1. Place the ice cream in a bowl. Wait until it has softened slightly and then pour in the cream and vanilla powder. Mix well.
  2. Place in the freezer again until it's time to serve.
  3. Heat the cloudberries and sugar in a saucepan. Then put in the liqueur and simmer on low heat for about 5 minutes.

Cloudberrie Pie

Ingredients

  1. 150g of butter
  2. 3dl wheat flour
  3. 1dl caster sugar
  4. 1tsp salt
  5. 0.5tsp baking powder
  6. 2.5dl cloudberryjam
  7. 2 dl cream
  8. 2-3tbsp punch

Do the following:

  1. Heat the oven to 225 degrees.
  2. Mix room-warm butter with the wheat flour, sugar, salt and baking powder.
  3. Spread the dough in a pie form, spread the cloudberryjam over the pie crust and bake for about 25-30 minutes
  4. Whip the cream lightly and turn the punch down. Serve the pie warm.
